**If you can copy and paste, you already know how to use this. Promise.**

AI tools like **ChatGPT** and **Claude** are like a super-smart helper who can plan, write, and brainstorm for you in seconds. The only catch is that they work best when you ask the *right* way.

So I did that part for you. Every prompt in here is already written. You just fill in a few blanks.

**The whole routine:**

1. **Pick a prompt** that matches what you need.
2. **Copy it all** — including the `[bracket parts]`.
3. **Swap the brackets** for your details (kids' ages, what's in your fridge, etc.).
4. **Paste and send.**
5. **Want changes?** Just ask in plain words. "Make it cheaper." "My kid is picky." Done.

**Three things to relax about:**

- 💬 **Talk to it like a person.** No special words needed.

### 2.1 — The Weeknight Dinner Plan
`⏱ Saves ~45 min of planning`

> Act as a practical meal planner for a busy family. Create a dinner plan for **[number]** nights this week. We're a family of **[number]**, including kids ages **[ages]**. Give me a list with a one-line description of each meal.

**What you'll get:** A clean list of dinners you can actually picture cooking.

💡 **Pro tip:** Love it? Reply with "Now make a grocery list organized by store aisle."

---

### 2.2 — Use What's Already in My Fridge

**What you'll get:** Creative meals that cut food waste and skip a store run.

💡 **Pro tip:** Perfect for the night before payday.

**What you'll get:** Low-pressure ideas built on foods your kid already trusts.

💡 **Pro tip:** Add "Give each meal a fun name a 5-year-old would be curious about."

## ⭐ BONUS — Bad Prompt vs. Good Prompt

**The secret nobody tells you: AI isn't bad — vague questions are.** Here's the difference, so you can write your own great prompts anytime.

---

**1. Meal ideas**
- 🚫 **Bad:** "Give me dinner ideas."
- ✅ **Good:** "Give me 5 kid-friendly dinners for a family of 4 that take under 30 minutes, no seafood, using a grocery budget of $60 for the week."
- **Why it's better:** Numbers, limits, and your real situation = answers you can actually use.

---

**2. Activities**
- 🚫 **Bad:** "What can my kids do?"
- ✅ **Good:** "Give me 3 indoor activities for kids ages 4 and 7 for a rainy afternoon, using only things in a normal house, low mess."
- **Why it's better:** Ages + setting + supplies + your mess tolerance = ideas that fit *your* day.

---

**3. Homework help**
- 🚫 **Bad:** "Help with fractions."
- ✅ **Good:** "Explain fractions to my 8-year-old using pizza as an example. Don't give homework answers — just help him understand. Then ask him one question to check."
- **Why it's better:** It teaches instead of cheats, and matches your kid's level.

---

**4. Schedule**
- 🚫 **Bad:** "Help me get organized."
- ✅ **Good:** "Here's everything happening this week: [list]. Organize it day-by-day, flag conflicts, and tell me what to prep ahead."
- **Why it's better:** You gave it *your* real week, so the plan is actually yours.

---

**5. A reply**
- 🚫 **Bad:** "Write an email."
- ✅ **Good:** "Write a short, warm but firm reply to this message from my child's coach: [paste]. Keep it under 4 sentences."
- **Why it's better:** Tone + length + the actual message = a reply you can send as-is.

---

> 💡 **The formula:** **Who it's for** + **what you want** + **your limits** (time, budget, ages, dislikes) + **how you want it** (short, simple, a list). Hit those and AI gives you gold every time.
